Chapter 1 - The Surprising Power of Atomic Habits

  • Forget about goals, focus on system instead.
  • Winners and losers have same goals.
  • Achieving a goal is only a momentary change.
  • Goals restrict your happiness.
  • Goals are at odd with long-term progress.
  • Habits are compund interest of self-improvement. Getting 1% better everyday counts for in the long run.
  • Habits are double-edged sword. They can work for you or against you, which is why understanding the details is essential.
  • Small changes often appears to make no difference until you cross a critical threshold. The most powerful outcomes of any compunding process are delayed. You need to be patient.
  • An atomic habit is a little habit that is part of a larger system. Just as atoms are the building blocks of molecules, atomic habits are the building blocks of remarkable results.
  • If you want better results, then forget about setting goals. Focus on your system instead.
  • You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your system.

Chapter 2 - How Your Habits Shape Your Identity (and Vice Versa)

  • Changing our habits is challenging for two reasons:
    • We try to change the wrong thing.
    • We try to change our habit in the wrong way.
  • Outcomes are about what you get, processes are about what you do and identity is about what you believe.
  • The ultimate form of motivation is when a habit becomes part of your identity.
  • Once your pride gets involved, you'll fight tooth and nail to maintain your habits.
  • True behaviour change is identity change.
  • Your behaviours are usually a reflection of your identity.
  • Good Habits can make rational sense, but if they conflict with your identity, you will fail to put them in action.
  • The more ypu repeat a behaviour, the more you reinforce the identity associated with that behaviour.
  • Your identity is literally your "repeated beingness".
