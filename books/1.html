Chapter 3 - How to Build Better Habits in 4 Simple Steps

  • Behaviours followed by satisfying consequences tend to be repeated and those that produce unpleasant consequences are less likely to be repeated.
  • The process of habit formation begins with trial and error.
  • The feedback loop behind all human behaviour: try, fail, learn, try differently.
  • The primary reason the brain remembers the past is to better predict what will work in the future.
  • Habits do not restrict freedom. They create it.
  • The process of building a habit can be divided into four simple steps: cue, craving, response and reward.
  • The cue triggers yout brain to initiate a behavior.
  • Cravings are the motivational force behind every habit.
  • Response is the actual habit you perform, which can take he form of a thought or an action.
  • Reward are the end goal of every habit.
  • A habit is a behavior that has been repeated enough times to become automatic.
  • The ultimate purpose of habits is to solve the problem of life as little energy and effort as possible.
  • Any habit can be broken down into a feedback loop that involves four steps: cue, craving, response and reward.
