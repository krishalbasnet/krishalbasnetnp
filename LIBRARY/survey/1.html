Introduction To Surveying

Definition of Surveying:
Surveying is defined as the art of determining the relative position of various points on, above or below the surface of the earth with the help of Linear and Angular measurements, both horizontal and vertical and presentation of these information either graphically or numerically.
Objectives of Surveing
  • To collect data to determine relative position of points on the surface of the earth
  • To prepare plan and map
  • For setting out works or lay out works
  • To find area and volume required for various purpose
Principle of Surveying
  • Working from whole to the part:

    According to the first principle, the whole area is first enclosed by main stations (controlling stations) and main survey lines (controlling lines). The area is then divided into a number of parts by forming well-conditioned triangles. The main survey lines are measured very accurately with a standard chain and then the sides of triangles are measured.
    The purpose of this process of working is to prevent accumulation of error. During this procedure, if there is any error in the measurement of any side of a triangle, then it will not affect the whole work. The error can always be detected and eliminated.
    But, if the reverse process (from the part to the whole) is followed, then the minor error in measurement will be magnified in the process of expansion and these errors will become absolutely uncontrollable.

  • Location of a point by measurements from two point of references:

    The relative position of various points (say point C) in survey works can be easily be located from two known fixed points (say A and B) by only one of the methods below:

    1. By measuring two distance d1 and d2 from two fixed points
    2. By measuring a perpendicular distance of C from line AB
    3. By measuring one angle Θ and one distance d
    4. By measuring two angles Θ1 and Θ2

Classification of Surveying based on accuracy
  • Plane Surveying:

    The Plane surveying is the type of surveying in which, the curvature of the earth is not considered and it is considered as a plane surface. The area surveyed should be less than 250 sq. km.

  • Geodetic Surveying:

    The Geodetic Surveying is the type of surveying in which, the curvature of the earth is considered. The object of the Geodetic surveying is to determine very precisely the relative or absolute positions on the earth's surface of a widely points.
