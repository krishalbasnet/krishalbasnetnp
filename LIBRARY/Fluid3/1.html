Fluid Mechanics - Basic Concepts and Definition

  1. Fluid Properties
    • Density :-

      The mass density of an object is defined as mass per unit volume. Density is generally denoted by ρ(rho) and is defined as mass of the substance per unit volume.
      ρ = mass / volume = m / v

    • Specific Weight :-

      Specific weight is generally denoted by γ(gamma) and defined as the ratio of weight per unit volume.
      γ= weight / volume = (mass * acceleration due to gravity) / volume = ρg

    • Specific Gravity

      Specific Gravity of a liquid is the ratio of its density to that of pure water at a standard temperature. It is generally denoted by S.
      S = γ liquid / γ water at 4oC = ρL * g /ρW * g = ρL/ρW

    • Specific Volume

      Specific Volume is the volume occupied by a unit mass of fluid. It is the reciprocal of mass density.
      Specific Volume = 1/ρ = volume / mass
